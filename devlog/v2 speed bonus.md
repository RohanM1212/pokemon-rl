This is devlog entry 2, looking at reward function v2. The big issue with the first version was that the agent did not care about time. A slow win and a fast win looked exactly the same to the agent. To fix this, I added a speed bonus to the calculation. Now, when the agent wins, it gets 0.1 additional reward for every single turn remaining out of the maximum 50. For example, if it wins the battle in 5 turns, it gets a massive bonus of 4.5 points. If it drags the fight out and wins on turn 49, it only gets a tiny 0.1 bonus.
The hypothesis was that this penalty on time would force the agent to play much more aggressively. I wanted it to use its strongest attacks immediately and stop wasting turns on things like items or switching out. What I expected to see in the logs was a much lower average turn count and a higher overall reward per episode.
What I found during the training run was that the speed bonus absolutely changed the agent. The number of turns per battle dropped quickly. The agent figured out that turns were basically currency, so it started attacking right out of the gate.
The thing is, it created a weird failure mode. The agent became completely reckless. Because the speed bonus was guaranteed for finishing early, the model started ignoring its own health entirely. It would happily take a massive hit that left it at low HP just to click a high power move and end the fight one turn faster. It was playing like a speedrunner who ignores their health bar because they are only watching the timer.
What actually happened is that the agent learned to value speed over safety. It won faster on average, but it also lost matches it easily could have won if it just played a bit more carefully. That tension between winning fast and staying healthy is exactly what is missing right now. It is a tough balancing act, and figuring out how to make the agent play smart instead of just fast is the main motivation for v3.
